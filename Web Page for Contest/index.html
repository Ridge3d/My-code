Frequently Asked Questions

  • How are you going to assign the Shuyukh to the packages, in other words, if I want to stay with a specific Shaykh, which package should I choose?

    • Sh. Muhammad Alshareef is in charge of the guidance and overall religious program, and conducting lectures and seminars. Sh. Muhammad will primarily be with the Deluxe package and Sh Abu Eesa will be leading the Student’s package, yet all the Shuyūkh and team leaders will be roaming between the groups, and we will all be together during the days of the Hajj rites themselves. Basically, regardless of what package you choose, in shā Allah you will get to spend the same time as all the participants with the Shuyūkh.

  • What about online courses?

    • Online courses will be offered through www.hajjcoach.com - the course offered to Al-Muyassar Hajj participants is an interactive exclusive version of the free course offered to the public. There will also be an online educational program through our own teleseminar software for the entire group before we leave.

  • When are you going to finalize the itinerary?

    • The itinerary is subject to many variables, for example the starting date of the month of Dhu'l-Hijjah etc. We are doing our best to finalize the itinerary as soon as possible. Please note, any changes made on the itinerary are going to be within the items mentioned on our website. Please keep checking the website for any updates.

  • I have heard that travelling times between cities can be quite lengthy. Will we be able to avoid this?

    • Regardless of what package you take, it's best to prepare yourself for long waits in traffic and the Mutawwif offices. We do our best to prepare the necessary documents for quick processing by the officials, but it's not uncommon to experience long waits.

  • Where is Shishah and how far is it from the Haram?

    • Shishah is a suburb of Makkah that is adjacent to Mina (next to Aziziyyah). The distance is roughly 5 km. Shishah is still considered to be in Makkah, and therefore is considered to be within the historical boundaries of the general Haram area.

  • If I'm staying at Shishah how can I get to the Haram if no shuttle is available at that time?

    • There are several taxis and buses that can take you from Aziziyyah to the Haram (and from the Haram to Aziziyyah). Unlike in the West, the transport prices aren't fixed. Before and after the Hajj, the typical cost is between 3-5 SAR (roughly $1 CAD) per person. During the busiest times, prices can be as high as 30 SAR per person or even higher, depending on how well you can bargain. In all cases, we recommend travelling in groups and never to travel alone, especially for women because it could be unsafe.

  • Why do you include Shishah in my package if it is far from the Haram?

    • It depends on your package. Prices for accommodation near the Haram are significantly higher than accommodation further from the Haram. For cheaper packages, including Shishah is essential to keep the cost of the package within reach of budget travellers; if you are taking any higher package and it includes Shishah beside other hotel accommodation in Makkah, then this is a huge bonus, since you will have access to Shisha accommodation (which is very near to Mina and the Jamarāt) during your stay in the Mina tents, meaning you can leave your luggage in Shisha. Likewise you will have access to your private shower facilities unlike the public showers in Mina.

  • What can we expect the weather to be like during our stay?

    • The Hajj season will be during the Autumn/fall of that region. During the day you can expect temperatures of up to 45ºC and during the night the temperature can drop to as low as 12ºC. It's very rare for there to be any rain in the region at this time however there were flooding incidents in the past due to poor sewage infrastructure. Please bring appropriate clothing to accommodate for this weather.

  • How much spending money should I bring?

    • One Canadian dollar is roughly equal to 3 Saudi riyals (one British pound is equal to 5-6 SAR). In terms of food prices, a typical sandwich from a street vendor cost from 3-5 SAR. From cleaner, more 'westernized' eateries, you can expect to pay roughly 25-40 SAR per person for a typical meal. Transportation and other goods will obviously vary in price depending on quality.

  • If I get lost, what do I do?

    • You will be provided the address of where you are staying at each stage of your trip. The name tag you will also have contact information on the back which you should be able to show to any official to help you find your way, in shā Allah.

  • What are the star-ratings of the hotels we will be using?

    • Star-ratings in Saudi Arabia use different standards to the one used in the West.We try to avoid using them since they are irrelevant to what we are accustomed to in North America.
